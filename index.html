Data Source: The data utilized for this analysis is sourced from the San Francisco Police Department Incident Reports: Historical 2003 to May 2018 and 2018 to Present, which are accessible on the url: https://data.sfgov.org/browse?category=Public+Safety(The data was selected until 2019 to take into account the impact of the emergence of the COVID-19 pandemic on the number of crimes)

We have analyzed the data concerning potential crime types associated with Prop-47. According to the bar graph, the following observations can be made:


  • Between 2003 and 2011, the crime rates remained relatively stable. However, after 2014, there has been a noticeable upward trend in overall crime rates, with the rate of increase becoming more pronounced.
  • LARCENY/THEFT constitutes the most prevalent crime type and significantly influences the overall crime rate. After the enactment of Prop-47, the number of LARCENY/THEFT crimes has consistently risen and exhibited significant fluctuations, closely mirroring the overall trend in crime rates.
  • The overall variations in the numbers of BURGLARY and FRAUD crimes have been minimal, fluctuating over time.
  • The counts of ROBBERY crimes has seen a slight decrease.

Support



  • The New York Times editorial board praised it as a way to reduce overcrowding in the state’s prisons.
  • The Los Angeles Times editorial board wrote that the measure is "a good and timely step that could help the state use its criminal justice and incarceration resources more wisely."
  • The American Civil Liberties Union also supported the measure and donated $3.5 million.


Oppositon



  • Under the law, possession of the date rape drug Rohypnol would be considered a misdemeanor rather than a felony.
  • Failure to use incarceration to force drug users into treatment will make it more difficult for drug users to accept treatment programs.
  • People don't just steal guns to add to their gun collections. They steal guns in order to commit another crime. But gun thieves are also protected by Proposition 47.
  • Proposition 47 required the release of thousands of dangerous inmates even when judges found the inmates were at risk of committing crimes such as kidnapping, robbery, assault, spousal abuse, animal cruelty, carjacking or committing felonies on behalf of a criminal street gang.
  • The probability of property snatchers targeting goods at specific price points has greatly increased. This not only exposes service industry employees to the risk of being harmed by property snatchers, but stores also have to spend more money to install anti-robbery equipment to protect goods.
  • California's retail boss lashed out, saying, "The problem now is that people who are addicted to drugs, have mental health issues, they will frankly go into stores and steal."

In conclusion, according to the dataset, there has been an increase in crimes such as theft, burglary and fraud, and a significant rise in crime rates in north-east part of San Francisco after the implementation of Proposition 47.

However, the current data does not definitively attribute the increase in crime solely to the enactment of this bill, nor can it directly conclude that the drawbacks of Proposition 47 outweigh its benefits. Nevertheless, it is an undeniable fact that many citizens are increasingly troubled and unsettled by the growing frequency of consequence-free criminal behavior(more thefts involving amounts less than $950).

Therefore, to thoroughly investigate the impact of the bill and propose new solutions, it is imperative to continue monitoring and conducting further data analysis. Subsequently, we can focus on whether there has been an increase in the number of repeat offenders targeted by Proposition 47 and whether the funds saved by Proposition 47 are truly being utilized to greater advantage.
